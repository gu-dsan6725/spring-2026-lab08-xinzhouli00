## Deduction Opportunities

### 1. Home Office Deduction

**What Qualifies:**
- Exclusive and regular business use of home space
- Simplified method: $5 per sq ft (max 300 sq ft = $1,500/year)
- Actual expense method: percentage of rent/mortgage, utilities, insurance
- Applies to remote workers, freelancers, and business owners

**IRS Reference:** Publication 587 | Schedule C | IRC Section 280A

**Estimated Annual Deduction:** $1,500-$8,000+

**Tax Savings (22% bracket):** $330-$1,760+

**Required Documentation:**
- Floor plan with office square footage
- Utility bills, rent/mortgage statements
- Photos of dedicated workspace (exclusive use required)

### 6. Health-Related Deductions (HSA, FSA, Medical)

**A. Health Savings Account (HSA) — Most Powerful Health Deduction:**
- 2025 Contribution Limits: $4,150 (individual) / $8,300 (family)
- Contributions are pre-tax (immediate deduction)
- Tax-free withdrawals for qualified medical expenses
- Unused funds roll over indefinitely — can invest like an IRA

**B. Medical Expenses (Schedule A):**
- Only expenses exceeding 7.5% of AGI are deductible
- Includes doctor visits, prescriptions, dental, vision, long-term care insurance

**C. Flexible Spending Account (FSA):**
- 2025 limit: $3,300 | Pre-tax contributions | "Use it or lose it" annually

**IRS Reference:** Publication 502 | Publication 969 | IRC Sections 223, 162(l)

### 7. Investment Tax-Loss Harvesting

**What Qualifies:**
- Realized losses from stocks, ETFs, mutual funds
- Losses offset gains dollar-for-dollar
- Up to $3,000 excess losses can offset ordinary income
- Unused losses carry forward indefinitely

**Key Rule:** Avoid repurchasing "substantially identical" security within 30 days (wash sale rule — IRC Section 1091)

**IRS Reference:** Schedule D | Publication 550 | IRC Section 1091

**Estimated Annual Tax Savings:**
- Conservative: $0-$220 (small losses)
- Moderate: $220-$1,100 (strategic harvesting)
- Aggressive: $1,100-$4,400+ (coordinated strategy)

**Required Documentation:** Brokerage statements with cost basis | Trade confirmations | 1099-B forms

### 8. Retirement Account Contributions

**A. Traditional IRA:**
- 2025 Limit: $7,000 ($8,000 if age 50+)
- Fully deductible if no employer plan; partially deductible with income limits
- **Tax Savings: $1,540/year (at 22%)**

**B. Solo 401(k) — Best for Self-Employed:**
- 2025 Limit: Up to $69,000 ($76,500 if 50+)
- Combines employee deferrals + employer profit-sharing contributions
- **Tax Savings: $4,400-$15,180/year**

**C. SEP-IRA:**
- Up to 25% of net self-employment income, max $69,000
- Simple to set up; excellent for high-income self-employed
- **Tax Savings: $1,100-$15,180/year**

**IRS Reference:** Publication 590-A | Publication 560 | IRC Sections 219, 401(k)

⚠️ **Deadline:** Contributions for 2025 taxes must be made by April 15, 2026

## Year-Round Tax Planning Tips

1. **Track in Real-Time, Not at Tax Season** — Use accounting software year-round. Digital receipts and photos of paper receipts prevent lost deductions and reduce audit risk.

2. **Know Your Standard Deduction Threshold** — 2025: $14,600 (single) / $29,200 (married). Track charitable donations and medical expenses quarterly to determine if itemizing beats the standard deduction.

3. **Maximize Retirement Contributions First** — IRA, 401(k), and SEP-IRA contributions provide the highest guaranteed return (tax savings + investment growth). Prioritize before other investments.

4. **Make Quarterly Estimated Tax Payments** — If self-employed or have significant side income, pay quarterly (April 15, June 15, Sept 15, Jan 15) to avoid underpayment penalties (owe more than $1,000 at filing).

5. **Keep Business and Personal Expenses Separate** — Maintain a dedicated business bank account and credit card. This simplifies Schedule C preparation, strengthens deduction legitimacy, and reduces audit risk significantly.
